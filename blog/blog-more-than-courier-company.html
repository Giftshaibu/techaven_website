Delivery is part of what Techaven does. It's not the purpose.

The problem Techaven solves isn't how to move a phone from one location to another. That problem has solutions: minibuses, courier services, or a friend with a vehicle. The problem is how a buyer in Malawi finds a legitimate electronics seller, pays safely, receives the right product, and has somewhere to turn if something goes wrong.

A courier handles the last step of that. Techaven handles all of it.

Image placeholder

Verified seller listings mean buyers aren't choosing between unknown options on scattered social media platforms. Escrow payments mean money isn't handed over before the buyer has leverage. In-house delivery keeps the physical movement of the product within the same accountability chain. Customer support and a dispute process mean the relationship doesn't end at the point of sale.

None of these components work particularly well in isolation. An escrow payment connected to a courier you can't trace is still a risk. A verified seller you pay directly without any escrow is still a risk. The model works because the pieces are connected.

For sellers, this is more than a delivery arrangement. Techaven provides a digital sales channel, product listing infrastructure, and access to buyers who are already in a payment-ready environment. That's a different value proposition from a courier that picks up and drops off.
